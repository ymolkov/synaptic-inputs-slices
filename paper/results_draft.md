## Synaptic Architecture and Interconnections

To decipher the effective synaptic connectivity between the key respiratory populations, we analyzed the magnitude of excitatory and inhibitory conductances during the inspiratory (Phase 0) and expiratory (Stationary) phases. Based on the phase-specificity of activity, we attribute inhibition during inspiration to **VGAT-I** neurons and inhibition during expiration to **VGAT-E** neurons. Excitation is attributed to **VgluT2-I** neurons and occurs during the **inspiratory** (Phase 0) phase, while excitatory inputs during expiration were found to be negligible.

The relative strengths of these synaptic inputs were classified as *strong* (largest conductance), *moderate* (second largest), or *weak* (third largest) for each population, as summarized in the schematic (Fig. X, where arrow thickness reflects input strength).

**VGAT-I Population**
These inhibitory inspiratory neurons received detailed phase-locked inputs. Contrary to simple reciprocal inhibition models, their strongest drive was excitatory input from VgluT2-I during the inspiratory phase ($0.2137 \pm 0.0347$ nS). However, they also received substantial inhibition during inspiration ($0.1736 \pm 0.0397$ nS), suggesting strong mutual or self-inhibition within the VGAT-I population. Inhibitory input from VGAT-E during expiration was present but weaker ($0.0805 \pm 0.0182$ nS).

**VgluT2-I Population**
The excitatory inputs to the purported rhythm-generating VgluT2-I population revealed a distinct pattern. The strongest drive was inhibition during expiration ($0.1766 \pm 0.0269$ nS) from VGAT-E, consistent with the hypothesis that post-inhibitory rebound plays a role in rhythmogenesis. VgluT2-I neurons received moderate excitatory input during the inspiratory phase ($0.1606 \pm 0.0177$ nS), indicative of recurrent excitation. Inhibition during inspiration (from VGAT-I) was found to be weaker ($0.1204 \pm 0.0283$ nS).

**VGAT-E Population**
Inhibitory expiratory neurons were dominated by strong inhibition during inspiration ($0.2863 \pm 0.0447$ nS) provided by VGAT-I, consistent with a reciprocal inhibition motif. They also receive moderate excitatory input from VgluT2-I during inspiration ($0.1331 \pm 0.0128$ nS). Recurrent inhibition during their active expiratory phase was weaker ($0.0544 \pm 0.0082$ nS).

In summary, the network architecture is characterized by strong inhibitory interactions—particularly reciprocal inhibition between inspiratory and expiratory populations—but also prominent excitatory drive to inhibitory neurons (VgluT2-I $\to$ VGAT-I) and recurrent excitation within the VgluT2-I population.
